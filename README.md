Car Price Analysis 

Day 1: Basic Analysis
Looked at the dataset and checked for missing data and duplicates.
Calculated average prices per car brand and model.
Made simple plots to compare prices by brand, fuel type, and horsepower.

Key Findings:
Some brands and models are much more expensive.
Diesel cars usually cost more than petrol cars.
Cars with more horsepower generally cost more.

Day 2: Advanced Analysis
Checked how features like engine size and horsepower relate to price.
Made scatter plots with trend lines for petrol and diesel cars.
Created interactive plots to explore prices easily.

Key Findings:
Bigger engines and higher horsepower mean higher prices.
Petrol cars are usually pricier, but diesel cars get expensive faster as power increases.
Interactive plots help compare cars and understand trends quickly.

Overall:
Car price depends mainly on brand, fuel type, engine size, and horsepower.
